REFERENCES OVERVIEW FOR RAG KNOWLEDGE BASE

These references were used to build the emotion and context guidance files.
Use them in the project report to justify that the RAG is grounded in official guidance and evidence-based mental wellbeing frameworks.

OFFICIAL / PUBLIC HEALTH SOURCES
1. World Health Organization. Doing What Matters in Times of Stress: An Illustrated Guide. Geneva: WHO; 2020.
   Official page: https://www.who.int/publications-detail-redirect/9789240003927
   WHO repository record: https://iris.who.int/handle/10665/331901

2. National Health Service (NHS). Every Mind Matters.
   Main page: https://www.nhs.uk/every-mind-matters/
   Low mood page: https://www.nhs.uk/every-mind-matters/mental-health-issues/low-mood/
   Anger page: https://www.nhs.uk/mental-health/feelings-symptoms-behaviours/feelings-and-symptoms/anger/

3. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). Emotional Well-Being.
   About: https://www.cdc.gov/emotional-well-being/about/index.html
   Improve: https://www.cdc.gov/emotional-well-being/improve-your-emotional-well-being/index.html
   Managing Difficult Emotions: https://www.cdc.gov/emotional-well-being/managing-difficult-emotions/index.html
   Sadness and Depression: https://www.cdc.gov/emotional-well-being/managing-difficult-emotions/sadness-depression.html

4. World Health Organization. Mental health.
   https://www.who.int/health-topics/mental-health

5. World Health Organization. Promoting well-being.
   https://www.who.int/activities/promoting-well-being

FOUNDATIONAL ACADEMIC SOURCES
6. Gross JJ. The emerging field of emotion regulation: an integrative review. Psychological Review. 1998;105(2):271-299.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295X.105.2.271

7. Beck AT. Cognitive Therapy and the Emotional Disorders. New York: Penguin / International Universities Press; 1979.

8. Hofmann SG, Asnaani A, Vonk IJJ, Sawyer AT, Fang A. The efficacy of cognitive behavioral therapy: a review of meta-analyses. World Psychiatry. 2012;11(3):427-440.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1002/wps.20002

NOTE FOR REPORTING
The guides are not copied verbatim from one source.
They are synthesized from official public-health guidance and established psychological frameworks,
then constrained to match the project design: short, supportive, non-diagnostic, context-aware nudges.
