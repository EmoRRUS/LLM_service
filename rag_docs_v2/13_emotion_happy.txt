EMOTION GUIDE: HAPPY

PURPOSE
Use this guide when the detected emotional state is happy.
The aim is to reinforce positive wellbeing and support savoring, gratitude, or meaningful continuation.

EVIDENCE BASIS
This guide is grounded in:
- CDC: About Emotional Well-Being
- WHO: Promoting well-being and mental health pages
- NHS Every Mind Matters: general wellbeing guidance
- Positive psychology background on reinforcing positive states

CORE INTERPRETATION
When the detected emotion is happy, the response should usually support the moment rather than interrupt it.
The message should help preserve the positive state and encourage healthy continuation.

RESPONSE GOALS
- Acknowledge the positive state naturally.
- Reinforce what is already working.
- Encourage savoring or gratitude when appropriate.
- Avoid turning a good moment into a problem-solving exercise.

RECOMMENDED SUPPORT DIRECTIONS
A good direction for happiness is to support staying with the good moment for a little longer.
A good direction for happiness is to help the user notice what is going well.
A good direction for happiness is to suggest sharing the moment when that feels natural.
A good direction for happiness is to carry the positive energy into a meaningful or nourishing next step.

GOOD ACTION TYPES
A good action for happiness is to pause for a moment and enjoy the feeling without rushing past it.
A good action for happiness is to keep doing what feels nourishing, meaningful, or steady.
A good action for happiness is to share the moment with someone when connection feels natural.
A good action for happiness is to mentally note what helped create the positive feeling.

STYLE RULES
- Use warm, natural, affirming wording.
- Keep it simple and grounded.
- Do not overpraise or sound exaggerated.

AVOID
- Over-analyzing the emotion.
- Adding unnecessary correction or warnings.
- Abruptly shifting into problem-solving.

CONTEXT ADAPTATION HINTS
When the user is at work, university, or another study setting, support for happiness can suggest carrying the steady energy into the next task.
When the user is at home or it is a weekend, support for happiness can more easily include enjoyment, connection, gratitude, or rest.
When the weather is clear and it is daytime, support for happiness can include outdoor appreciation when that is practical.
When it is night time, support for happiness can lean toward softer appreciation and calm continuation.
