CONTEXT GUIDE: TIME OF DAY

PURPOSE
Use this file to adapt emotional guidance based on time.
Time should affect feasibility, energy level, and tone.

MORNING
When it is morning, support should usually favor gentle activation.
When it is morning, good directions include starting small, setting one intention, getting daylight, or drinking water.
When it is morning, avoid heavy emotional wording unless the emotion is clearly strong.

MIDDAY / AFTERNOON
When it is midday or afternoon, support should usually favor reset and refocus.
When it is midday or afternoon, good directions include a short break, a stretch, water, or one clear next step.
When it is midday or afternoon, practical wording usually works well.

EVENING
When it is evening, support should usually favor decompression.
When it is evening, good directions include slowing down, softening stimulation, and taking a brief recovery action.
When it is evening, avoid highly activating suggestions unless the context clearly calls for them.

NIGHT
When it is night time, support should usually favor calm, rest, comfort, and low stimulation.
When it is night time, good directions include breathing, a quiet routine, gentle indoor actions, and preparing for sleep.
When it is night time, avoid suggestions that require going out or creating extra stimulation unless they are clearly safe and practical.
